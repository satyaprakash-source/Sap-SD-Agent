⟳ Lead-to-Cash Business Process
Module 1 · T-Code: Theory
Pending ▼
The Lead-to-Cash (L2C) process is the complete end-to-end business cycle in SAP SD. It represents how a company captures revenue from a customer relationship.

Key stages: Lead → Inquiry → Quotation → Sales Order → Delivery → Picking → Goods Issue → Billing → Payment.

❓ Difference between Inquiry and Quotation?

🏢 Organizational Levels
Module 2 · T-Code: SPRO / OX15
Pending ▼
SAP SD organizational structure:
• Client: Highest level
• Company Code: Legal entity (FI integration)
• Sales Organization: Responsible for selling
• Distribution Channel: How goods reach customer
• Division: Product line grouping
• Sales Area = Sales Org + Dist Channel + Division
SPRO / OX15
Practice: In SPRO → Enterprise Structure, define a new Sales Organization. Assign it to your Company Code. Create a Sales Area.

📦 Master Data — Material Master
Module 3a · T-Code: MM03
Pending ▲
Material Master is the central repository for material-related data. For SD, key views are:
• Sales: Sales Org 1 & 2 (delivering plant, item category group)

💰 Pricing Configuration
Module 4 · T-Code: VK11
Pending ▼
SAP pricing uses the Condition Technique:
1. Condition Types (PR00 = base price, K007 = discount)
2. Condition Tables (where price is stored)
3. Access Sequence (search order)
4. Pricing Procedure (combines all condition types)

Determined by: Sales Area + Customer Pricing Proc + Document Pricing Proc.
VK11
Practice: Use VK11 to create a price condition for PR00. Enter your material number and set a price. Then create a sales order (VA01) and verify the price is auto-determined.
INTERVIEW QUESTIONS
❓ What is condition technique?
❓ What is a pricing procedure?
❓ How is pricing procedure determined?
❓ Explain PR00 condition type.

🚚 Delivery Processing
Module 6 · T-Code: VL01N / VL02N
Pending ▼
Delivery processing steps:
1. Create Outbound Delivery (VL01N)
2. Picking: Transfer order to pick goods from warehouse
3. Pack: Optional packaging
4. Post Goods Issue (PGI) via VL02N

PGI impact: Stock reduced, Delivery complete, COGS posted in FI. Billing only possible AFTER PGI.
VL01N / VL02N
Practice: Create delivery using VL01N referencing your sales order. Enter delivery date and shipping point. Open VL02N, perform picking, then Post Goods Issue. Verify stock reduced.
INTERVIEW QUESTIONS

❓ What happens in FI when PGI is posted?

🧾 Billing & Invoice
Module 7 · T-Code: VF01
Pending ▼
Billing is the final step in Order-to-Cash:
1. Create Invoice using VF01 — references delivery document
2. Billing generates Accounting Document in FI (Dr Customer, Cr Revenue)
3. Customer account is debited
4. Revenue is recognized

Billing types: F2 (invoice), G2 (credit memo), L2 (debit memo), RE (returns).
VF01
Practice: Create customer invoice using VF01 referencing your delivery document. Save and display the accounting document. Verify Customer Account debited and Revenue Account credited.
INTERVIEW QUESTIONS
❓ What accounting entries are created when billing?
❓ Difference between F2 and G2 billing types?
❓ How does billing integrate with FI?
❓ What is billing due list (VF04)?

Interview Preparation

Real questions, ideal answers, mock interviews

⚡ Readiness: 12%
Interview Question Bank
Q1What is Sales Area in SAP SD?
›
BASICS
Sales Area is the combination of Sales Organization + Distribution Channel + Division. It is the most important organizational element in SD. Every sales transaction must be assigned to a Sales Area.
Q2What is a Pricing Procedure?
›
CONFIG
A Pricing Procedure is a sequence of condition types (like PR00, K007, MWST) arranged in order with calculation rules. Determined by: Sales Area + Customer Pricing Procedure + Document Pricing Procedure.
Q3What is Copy Control in SAP SD?
›
CONFIG
Copy Control defines how data is copied from one document to another. Controls which fields are copied, which are re-determined. Configured in VTAA (order to order), VTLA (order to delivery), VTFL (delivery to billing).
Q4What is a Schedule Line?
›
PROCESS
A Schedule Line is a line within a sales order item specifying the confirmed delivery quantity and delivery date. One order item can have multiple schedule lines for partial deliveries.
Q5Explain the Lead-to-Cash process.
›
PROCESS
Lead → Inquiry (VA11) → Quotation (VA21) → Sales Order (VA01) → Outbound Delivery (VL01N) → Picking → Post Goods Issue (VL02N) → Billing/Invoice (VF01) → Payment. Each step creates a linked document.
Q6What is Post Goods Issue (PGI)?
›
PROCESS
PGI is the most critical step in delivery. When posted: Stock reduced in SAP, Goods Issue accounting entry posted (Dr COGS, Cr Inventory), Delivery status changes, Billing becomes possible. Done via VL02N.
Q7What is the condition technique?
›
CONFIG
Condition Technique is the backbone of SAP SD pricing. Consists of: Condition Types → Condition Tables → Access Sequences → Pricing Procedures. Used for: pricing, taxes, output determination, account determination.
Q8What is Item Category in SAP SD?
›
ADVANCED
Item Category controls how a sales order item is processed. Determines delivery/billing relevance, pricing. Key types: TAN (standard), TAQ (quotation), TAB (third-party). Determined by: Sales Doc Type + Item Category Group.
Q9What is Credit Management?
›
ADVANCED
Controls customer credit exposure. Key elements: Credit Control Area (FI), Credit Limit (FD32), Credit Check. When exceeded, order gets credit block released only by credit manager (VKM3).
Q10Difference between Inquiry and Quotation?
›
BASICS
Inquiry (VA11) is a non-binding customer request for information. Quotation (VA21) is a legally binding offer with specific prices, quantities, and validity dates that can be referenced when creating a sales order.
Q11How does SD integrate with FI in billing?
›
ADVANCED
When billing document is saved (VF01), it auto-creates an accounting document in FI: Debit Customer Account and Credit Revenue Account. Revenue Account Determination (VKOA) maps condition types to G/L accounts.
Q12What is an Outbound Delivery?
›
PROCESS
Outbound Delivery (VL01N) initiates physical movement of goods to customer. Copies data from sales order and allows warehouse processing (picking, packing). Goods Issue must be posted before billing.
